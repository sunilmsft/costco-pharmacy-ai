### Message 2: Sunil's follow-up email to Director/AVP

Hi [Name],

Thank you for being open to taking a look.

I wanted to share a small concept exploration around something I'm deeply passionate about: making complex customer journeys feel simpler, more guided, and more human.

A lot of my recent product work has focused on improving AI-assisted customer engagement experiences from the customer's point of view — not adding AI for the sake of it, but thinking through where it can reduce friction, improve clarity, and help people get to the right next step. I've also been experimenting with multilingual digital assistants and guided online journeys for small businesses, which has reinforced how much opportunity there is when technology feels approachable and genuinely helpful.

Because Costco Pharmacy is a space my family is close to, I explored a simple question:

What if an intelligent experience layer could help pharmacy members find the right next step with more clarity, confidence, and care?

This is not meant as a critique of the current Costco Pharmacy site, or as a proposal to replace or rebuild anything. It is also not about replacing pharmacists, pharmacy staff, or existing pharmacy workflows. The starting point is that Costco Pharmacy already has a strong digital foundation and a broad set of useful member services.

The concept is more about how an always-on guided layer could complement the existing experience by helping members navigate common moments like refills, transfers, immunizations, fulfillment options, family needs, and service discovery — especially when they are unsure where to begin or need simple next-step guidance outside normal pharmacy hours.

I realize something like this would be much bigger than any one person and would require the right internal ownership across pharmacy, compliance, privacy, technology, and business teams. My hope is simply to share the thinking as a conversation starter and get your honest reaction on whether this kind of experience pattern feels useful, relevant to Costco's member experience, and worth exploring further with the right teams.
